How It Works

Simple, Private, On-Device

MindfulTime uses Apple's Family Controls framework — no account required, no data leaves your phone.

1

Grant Screen Time Access

On first launch, MindfulTime asks for Screen Time permission using Apple's Family Controls API. This is the only permission needed.

2

Set Your Goals

Choose which apps you want to limit and set a daily time budget. MindfulTime tracks your progress throughout the day.

3

Activate Focus When You Need It

Start a Focus Mode session to shield distracting apps using Managed Settings. No VPN, no MDM — it just works.

4

Review & Improve

Your home dashboard shows daily and weekly trends. Over time, you'll build habits that reduce mindless scrolling.

FAQ

Questions, Answered

The things prospective users ask us most.

How is MindfulTime different from iOS Screen Time?
iOS Screen Time shows you how much you used; MindfulTime helps you change it. You set personalized goals around the apps that actually matter — not calls and texts — with flexible weekday, weekend, and evening schedules and smart nudges that keep you on track. Choose Mindful mode for gentle accountability or Committed mode to block apps when you mean business, and stay motivated with streaks and achievements along the way. (See the comparison above for a side-by-side.)
Is my data private?
Yes. Everything stays on your device. MindfulTime has no account, no servers, and no third-party analytics or tracking — your Screen Time data is never uploaded, shared, or sold. It's built entirely on Apple's on-device frameworks.
Does it cost anything? Are there ads?
No ads, no tracking, no subscriptions, and no in-app purchases.
Does MindfulTime block apps?
Only if you want it to. Set a goal to Committed mode and the apps you choose are restricted once you hit your limit. You can also start a Focus Mode session — like Deep Work or Digital Detox — to shield distracting apps for its duration. Blocking uses Apple's Managed Settings (no VPN, no MDM). Prefer awareness over restriction? Use Mindful mode and nothing is blocked.
What do I need to use it?
An iPhone running iOS 16 or later, and Screen Time permission — granted on first launch via Apple's Family Controls. No account or sign-up required.
Does it replace iOS Screen Time?
No — it complements it. MindfulTime is built on Apple's Screen Time framework and layers personalized goals, schedules, nudges, and motivation on top. You can keep using Apple's built-in limits alongside it.

Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 2026

MindfulTime ("the app", "we", "our") is committed to protecting your privacy. This policy explains what data the app accesses, how it is used, and how it is protected.

1. Data We Access

MindfulTime requests access to Screen Time data via Apple's Family Controls framework. This includes:

  • Daily and weekly app usage durations
  • App pickup counts and notification counts
  • App and category breakdowns provided by the DeviceActivity framework

2. How Data Is Used

Screen Time data is used exclusively to:

  • Display your personal usage statistics within the app
  • Track your progress toward self-defined screen time goals
  • Send you optional local notifications about your usage
  • Enable Focus Mode app shielding via Apple's Managed Settings framework

3. Data Storage

All data is stored locally on your device using Apple's on-device APIs (UserDefaults, App Groups, and DeviceActivity). No usage data, personal information, or analytics are transmitted to any external server. MindfulTime has no backend, no user accounts, and no cloud storage.

4. Data Sharing

We do not share, sell, or transmit any data to third parties. Your Screen Time data is never sent outside your device.

5. Third-Party Services

MindfulTime does not use any third-party analytics, advertising SDKs, or tracking libraries. The app has no in-app purchases, ads, or subscriptions.

6. Children's Privacy

MindfulTime is intended for personal use by individuals aged 13 and older. The app does not knowingly collect data from children under 13.

7. Your Control

You can revoke Screen Time access at any time via Settings → Screen Time on your iPhone. You can also reset all app data from the Profile tab within the app, which clears all locally stored goals, focus modes, and preferences.

8. Changes to This Policy

If this privacy policy changes, the updated version will be posted on this page with a revised date. Continued use of the app after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

9. Contact

If you have any questions about this privacy policy, please contact us at:
